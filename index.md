# An Exploration Starting from Lived Experience
## An Open Inquiry into Social Common Ground

---

## English (U.S. & European Context)

This is an exploration of **social common ground**.

It does not require ideological agreement.  
Instead, it seeks to **lay out the full reasoning process step by step**,  
in order to examine the following question:

> In a world marked by deep division and persistent disagreement,  
> is it still possible to identify a **minimal set of principles**  
> that allow society to **coexist and function smoothly**?

We do not begin with abstract theory,  
but with **lived, concrete, repeatedly observed experience**.

Because if a principle cannot respond to real, felt pressure,  
it cannot be understoodâ€”let alone accepted.

---

## What This Project Does

This site proceeds along a **deliberate and restrained path**:

### Starting from lived experience  
Describing widely shared pressures without rushing to assign blame

### Seeking coexistence amid differences  
Addressing *how not to overpower one another* before debating *who is right*

### Relying on reasoned arguments rather than emotional mobilization  
Allowing different explanations to be compared on their own logical grounds

### Identifying and comparing sources of problems and institutional paths  
Including their explanatory power, costs, and long-term sustainability

### Gradually deriving minimal social principles  
Not a blueprint for an ideal society,  
but a **floor that prevents systemic deterioration and enables stable operation**

---

## What This Project Does Not Do

- It does not offer belief systems  
- It rejects coercion  
- It does not persuade through moral superiority  
- It does not deny conflict, but refuses to turn conflict into domination

---

## Why Begin with Lived Pressure

Social common ground does not emerge from slogans.  
It takes shape through **shared problems that people actually experience**.

That is why this exploration begins here:

**Let us first describe the problems many people are facing.  
You can consider whether these reflect the society you observe.**
