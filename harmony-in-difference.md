## English (U.S. & European Context)

# Harmony in Difference
## â€” A Practical Mechanism for Coexistence

After acknowledging a set of widely felt conditions,
a more fundamental and difficult question arises:

> **If the causes of these conditions are widely disputed,  
> how can we move, step by step, toward more reasonable explanations?**

Before asking *what the causes are* or *which explanation is correct*,
we must first address a deeper issue.

---

## 1. Disagreement Is Not Accidental

When social reality is complex, layered, and changing,
different people naturally form different explanations:

- They occupy different positions  
- They bear different risks  
- They observe different causal links  
- They rely on different information and language  

For this reason, **disagreement is not a failure**,  
but a natural outcome of complex reality.

Forcing a single explanation too early
often obscures rather than clarifies what is real.

---

## 2. The Problem Is Not Disagreement, but How It Is Handled

The real issue is not:

- Why different views exist  

But rather:

- **How we treat those differences**

When disagreement is treated as something to be eliminated,
common outcomes follow:

- Certain views are labeled as illegitimate  
- Authority, emotion, or identity is used to suppress others  
- Complex questions are reduced to opposing camps  

These approaches may quiet conflict temporarily,
but they do so at a cost:

> **Comparison stops.  
> Revision stops.  
> And progress toward understanding stops as well.**

---

## 3. Harmony in Difference as a Methodological Precondition

â€œHarmony in differenceâ€ is not a call to abandon judgment,
nor a demand for value uniformity.

It establishes a **methodological precondition**:

> **No explanation should be excluded  
> before it has been examined and compared.**

Under this condition:

- Differences are treated as objects of inquiry, not threats  
- Multiple explanations can coexist without mutual elimination  
- The focus shifts from *who is aligned with whom*  
  to *which explanation withstands comparison*  

This is not a concession to any position.
It is a prerequisite for rational evaluation itself.

---

## 4. Why Comparison Requires Coexistence

From an epistemic standpoint,
comparison requires at least three conditions:

1. Explanations can be fully expressed  
2. They can be repeatedly compared  
3. Revision does not immediately trigger punishment  

If holding a view means:

- Being defined as the problem itself  
- Being excluded from discussion  
- Or being treated as an adversary to be overcome  

Then rational behavior shifts from seeking truth to self-defense.

In such an environment,
people do not ask *â€œAm I wrong?â€*  
They ask *â€œAm I safe?â€*

---

## 5. Coexistence Is Not the End, but the Beginning

It must be emphasized:

**Harmony in difference does not solve the problem itself.**

It does not tell us:

- Which explanation is correct  
- Which institution is superior  
- Which path will succeed  

It merely provides a minimal foundation:

> **That discussion can continue  
> without immediately collapsing into domination or suppression.**

Without this foundation,
any â€œanswerâ€ is more likely to become
a new source of conflict.

---

## 6. What Follows Once Coexistence Is Secured

Once we accept that:

- Disagreement is normal  
- Coexistence is a prerequisite  
- Forced uniformity undermines comparison  

The next question naturally arises:

> **If explanations can coexist,  
> by what means should they be evaluated?**

Emotion, identity, and alignment
are insufficient for this task.

This leads directly to the next stage of exploration.

---
